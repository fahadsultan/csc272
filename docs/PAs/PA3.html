American Community Survey (ACS) is an ongoing survey conducted by the U.S. Census Bureau. It collects detailed demographic, social, economic, and housing data from a representative sample of the U.S. population. The data is used to produce estimates of the population for states, counties, cities, and other geographic units.

Question 1. Percent Change in Median Income per Capita

Identify 10 counties with the most increase and 10 counties with the most decrease in Income per capita from 2015 to 2017.

Your answer must be a dataframe have the shape (20, 3)

  • 20 rows:
    • the top 10 rows representing the counties with the most increase and
    • the bottom 10 rows representing the counties with the most decrease
  • 3 columns: County, State, Change in Income per Capita

The Percent Change in Income per Capita is the difference between the Income per Capita in 2017 and 2015.

\[ \text{Percent Change in Income per Capita } = 100 \times \frac{\text{Income per Capita in 2017} - \text{Income per Capita in 2015}}{\text{Income per Capita in 2015}} \]

where Income per Capita is calculated as follows:


\[ \text{Income per Capita} = \frac{\text{Income}}{\text{Total Population}} \]


Note that the Income and Total Population are given in the data for each county.
